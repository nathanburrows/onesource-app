Bookmarklets

Drag buttons to your bookmarks bar. Use them on any Dynamics 365 or NICE SPM page. auto-refreshes every 20s

CRM Entry
📥 CRM-Import

Click anywhere in Dynamics. Pulls every account where you're owner OR any technology rep — plus ALL opps, ERRs, and activities (calls, emails, appts, tasks; last 12mo) on those accounts. ~252 accounts, ~10–30 sec.

Sales Data
📊 NICE-Import

From any NICE SPM page, click this. Opens a popup, runs both the Transactions and Margin Adjustments reports for the current fiscal year, scrapes both, posts to local proxy. ~30–90 sec.

📊 OSIT-Sync

From an OSIT Quote Search dialog (open it via the "Open Quote" button in OSIT first), click this. Prompts for one or more rep IDs (comma-separated; defaults to your last entry) plus a date range. For each rep, drives the OSIT search UI — clears userIdSearch, fills the rep + dates in the Additional Options panel, clicks Search, waits for the grid, then fetches the export inline and POSTs all rows to the OneSourceCMD proxy. No CSV download, no manual upload.

How to install

1. Show your bookmarks bar (if hidden): Chrome menu → Bookmarks → Show Bookmarks Bar — or press ⌘ Shift B.
2. Drag the green CRM-Import button above onto your bookmarks bar.
3. Make sure the local proxy is running — double-click start-onesource.command (or start-onesource-olama.command) in the project folder.
4. Use it. Click anywhere in Dynamics → click CRM-Import in your bookmarks bar. A popup opens, pulls all your accounts and their opps, posts to the proxy, and auto-closes.

What gets scraped

  • All open opportunities — name, stage, est value, est close date, probability, OEM/vendor, practice area, solution area, renewal value, project type, previous order numbers, owner, contact (top 5000)
  • Recent closed opportunities (last 3 years) — name, won/lost outcome, actual value, close date, owner (top 5000)
  • ERRs (Engineering Review Requests) — name, OpenAir project number, engineers, linked opportunity (top 5000)

The proxy keeps the snapshot in memory keyed by customer name and persists it to crm-cache.json in the project folder. The AI assistant's crm_lookup tool reads it on demand.

Why a popup?

Microsoft Dynamics' page sets a strict connect-src CSP that silently blocks any fetch() to non-Microsoft hosts — including https://localhost. A top-level form submission inside a popup window isn't subject to that CSP, so the bookmarklet opens a small popup and uses an auto-submitting form to deliver the payload.
